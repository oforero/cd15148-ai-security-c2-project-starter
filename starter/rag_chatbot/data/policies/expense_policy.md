# FinanceGuard Inc. — Expense Reimbursement Policy

**Effective Date:** January 1, 2025
**Policy Owner:** Finance Department
**Applies To:** All full-time and part-time employees

## Approved Expense Categories

Employees may submit reimbursement requests for the following business-related expenses:

- **Meals & Entertainment:** Business meals with clients or colleagues are reimbursable up to $75 per person per meal. Team meals for groups of 4+ require director pre-approval. Alcohol is reimbursable only when accompanying a client meal and must not exceed 20% of the total bill.

- **Transportation:** Rideshare (Uber, Lyft), taxi, public transit, and mileage reimbursement at the current IRS rate ($0.67/mile for 2025). Airport parking is reimbursable for trips under 7 days; longer trips require shuttle or offsite lot.

- **Office Supplies:** Individual purchases up to $200 do not require pre-approval. Bulk orders or equipment over $200 must be routed through the Procurement team.

- **Professional Development:** Conference registration, training courses, and certification exams are reimbursable with manager approval. Annual cap of $3,000 per employee.

- **Client Gifts:** Gifts to clients are reimbursable up to $100 per recipient per quarter. Gifts to government officials are prohibited under our Anti-Corruption Policy.

## Approval Hierarchy

| Amount | Required Approver |
|--------|------------------|
| $0 – $500 | Direct Manager |
| $501 – $2,000 | Department Director |
| $2,001 – $10,000 | Vice President |
| $10,001+ | CFO |

## Documentation Requirements

All reimbursement claims must include:
1. An itemized receipt (credit card statements alone are not sufficient)
2. Business purpose written on the receipt or in the submission notes
3. Names of attendees (for meals and entertainment)
4. Date and location of the expense

## Non-Reimbursable Items

The following are explicitly excluded: personal travel upgrades, gym memberships, home office furniture (unless pre-approved under the Remote Work Policy), parking tickets, fines, or penalties, and any expense submitted more than 60 days after it was incurred.

## Policy Violations

First violation: Written warning and mandatory policy refresher training. Second violation: 90-day suspension of reimbursement privileges. Third violation: Disciplinary action up to and including termination. Fraudulent claims will be reported to Legal and may result in criminal referral.
