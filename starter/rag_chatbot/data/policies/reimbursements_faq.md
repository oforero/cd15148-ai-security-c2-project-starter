# FinanceGuard Inc. — Expense Reimbursement FAQ

## General Questions

**Q: How long does reimbursement take after I submit my report?**
A: Standard processing time is 5–7 business days from the date of final approval. Direct deposit reimbursements appear in your next payroll cycle after processing. Paper checks (if requested) take an additional 3–5 business days for mailing.

**Q: I lost my receipt. Can I still get reimbursed?**
A: For expenses under $25, you may submit a Lost Receipt Affidavit form (available on the Finance intranet page) with a description of the expense, the approximate amount, and your manager's signature. For expenses $25 and above, an original or digital receipt is required — credit card statements alone are not accepted. Contact the vendor to request a duplicate receipt.

**Q: Can I split an expense across multiple cost centers?**
A: Yes. When submitting in Concur, use the "Split Expense" feature to allocate costs to different cost centers or project codes. Each split must have a valid cost center and be approved by the respective cost center owner.

**Q: How does currency conversion work for international expenses?**
A: Concur automatically converts foreign currencies using the exchange rate on the transaction date. If you paid in cash, attach a screenshot of the exchange rate from xe.com or similar on the date of purchase. Do not use your bank's conversion rate, as it may include fees.

**Q: Should I use my corporate card or pay out of pocket?**
A: Use your corporate credit card for all business expenses whenever possible. This simplifies reconciliation, provides automatic receipt matching in Concur, and ensures the company receives negotiated payment terms. Out-of-pocket expenses are reimbursable but require additional documentation and take longer to process.

## Specific Situations

**Q: My flight was canceled and I had to rebook at a higher fare. What do I do?**
A: Submit both the original booking confirmation and the rebooking receipt. Add a note explaining the cancellation. Airline-caused rebookings are always reimbursable regardless of fare difference. Weather or personal rebookings follow standard approval limits.

**Q: Can I expense a co-working space while traveling?**
A: Daily co-working passes (e.g., WeWork day pass) are reimbursable when you are traveling and need a workspace. Monthly memberships require Remote Work Policy approval and are not covered under the Travel Policy.

**Q: What if my manager is unavailable to approve my expense report?**
A: Expense reports not approved within 5 business days are automatically escalated to the next level in the approval chain. You may also designate an alternate approver in Concur under Settings → Delegates. The Finance team can manually re-route urgent reports — email expenses@financeguard.com.
